# Connecticut Data Privacy Act (CTDPA)

## Overview
The Connecticut Data Privacy Act (CTDPA), enacted in 2022 and effective July 1, 2023, is Connecticut's comprehensive consumer privacy law. CTDPA provides privacy protections for Connecticut residents and includes provisions for automated decision-making and profiling.

## Key Provisions

### Consumer Rights
- **Right to Access**: Consumers can request access to personal data
- **Right to Correct**: Consumers can correct inaccurate personal data
- **Right to Delete**: Consumers can request deletion of personal data
- **Right to Data Portability**: Consumers can obtain copies of personal data
- **Right to Opt-Out**: Consumers can opt out of targeted advertising, sale, and profiling

### Covered Entities
Businesses that:
- Conduct business in Connecticut or produce products/services targeted to Connecticut residents, AND
- During a calendar year, control or process personal data of at least 100,000 consumers, OR
- Control or process personal data of at least 25,000 consumers and derive over 25% of gross revenue from the sale of personal data

## AI and Automated Decision-Making

### Profiling and Automated Decisions
- **Opt-Out Rights**: Consumers can opt out of profiling for decisions with legal or similarly significant effects
- **Meaningful Information**: Right to meaningful information about automated decision-making logic
- **Human Review**: Right to human review of automated decisions
- **Transparency Requirements**: Controllers must explain automated processing

### AI System Obligations
- **AI Development**: Personal data used in AI development must comply with CTDPA
- **Model Training**: AI model training data protection requirements
- **Algorithmic Processing**: AI systems processing Connecticut resident data
- **Inference Protection**: AI-generated inferences about consumers are covered

## Data Protection Assessments

### Required Assessments
Data protection assessments required for processing that presents heightened risk, including:
- **Targeted Advertising**: Processing for behavioral advertising purposes
- **Sale of Personal Data**: Processing involving data sales
- **Profiling**: Profiling with legal or similarly significant effects
- **Sensitive Data**: Processing of sensitive personal data

### Assessment Components
- **Risk Identification**: Identifying potential risks to consumers
- **Impact Analysis**: Analyzing potential negative impacts
- **Benefit Assessment**: Weighing benefits against risks
- **Safeguard Implementation**: Implementing measures to mitigate risks

## Enforcement and Penalties

### Connecticut Attorney General
- **Exclusive Enforcement**: Connecticut Attorney General has sole enforcement authority
- **Investigation Powers**: Authority to investigate CTDPA violations
- **Civil Penalties**: Civil penalties for violations
- **Injunctive Relief**: Court orders to stop violations and ensure compliance

### Cure Period
- **60-Day Cure**: Businesses have 60 days to cure violations after notice
- **Good Faith Efforts**: Must make reasonable good faith efforts to cure
- **Penalty Protection**: Cure eliminates civil penalty liability for that violation
- **Documentation**: Must document cure efforts and completion

### No Private Right of Action
- **State Enforcement Only**: Only Connecticut Attorney General can enforce CTDPA
- **No Individual Lawsuits**: Consumers cannot sue directly under CTDPA
- **Other Legal Remedies**: Other laws may still provide private causes of action
- **Regulatory Approach**: Focus on regulatory rather than private enforcement

## Business Compliance Requirements

### Privacy Notice Requirements
- **Clear Disclosures**: Reasonably accessible and clear privacy notices
- **Data Categories**: Categories of personal data collected and processed
- **Processing Purposes**: Purposes for collecting and processing personal data
- **Third-Party Sharing**: Information about data sharing and disclosure
- **Consumer Rights**: Explanation of consumer rights and exercise procedures

### Consumer Request Processing
- **45-Day Response**: Must respond to consumer requests within 45 days
- **Identity Verification**: Reasonable procedures to verify consumer identity
- **Free Processing**: Generally must process requests without charge
- **Appeal Rights**: Must provide process for consumers to appeal denials
- **Record Keeping**: Maintain records of consumer requests and responses

### Data Processing Requirements
- **Data Minimization**: Collect only data necessary for disclosed purposes
- **Purpose Limitation**: Process data only for disclosed and compatible purposes
- **Retention Limits**: Retain personal data only as long as necessary
- **Security Safeguards**: Implement appropriate technical and organizational safeguards
- **Processor Contracts**: Appropriate agreements with data processors
