# Montana Consumer Data Privacy Act (MCDPA)

## Overview
The Montana Consumer Data Privacy Act (MCDPA), enacted in 2023 and effective October 1, 2024, is Montana's comprehensive consumer privacy law. MCDPA provides privacy protections for Montana residents and follows the model established by Virginia, Colorado, and Connecticut privacy laws.

## Key Provisions

### Consumer Rights
- **Right to Access**: Consumers can request access to personal data
- **Right to Correct**: Consumers can correct inaccurate personal data
- **Right to Delete**: Consumers can request deletion of personal data
- **Right to Data Portability**: Consumers can obtain copies of personal data
- **Right to Opt-Out**: Consumers can opt out of targeted advertising, sale, and profiling

### Covered Entities
Businesses that:
- Conduct business in Montana or produce products/services targeted to Montana residents, AND
- During a calendar year, control or process personal data of at least 50,000 consumers, OR
- Control or process personal data of at least 25,000 consumers and derive over 25% of gross revenue from the sale of personal data

## Unique Features

### Lower Thresholds
- **50,000 Consumer Threshold**: Lower than most other state privacy laws (typically 100,000)
- **Broader Coverage**: Covers more businesses due to lower thresholds
- **Revenue Threshold**: 25% revenue threshold for data sales (lower than some states)
- **Montana Focus**: Specifically protects Montana residents and their data

### Simplified Approach
- **Streamlined Requirements**: Fewer complex requirements than some comprehensive privacy laws
- **Business-Friendly**: Designed to be manageable for smaller businesses
- **Clear Obligations**: Clear and straightforward compliance requirements
- **Practical Implementation**: Practical approach to privacy compliance

## AI and Machine Learning Applications

### Automated Decision-Making
- **Profiling Rights**: Consumers can opt out of profiling for decisions with legal or similarly significant effects
- **AI Transparency**: Requirements for meaningful information about automated decision-making
- **Human Review**: Right to human review of automated decisions
- **AI Development**: Personal data used in AI development must comply with MCDPA

### AI System Obligations
- **Training Data**: Personal data used to train AI models is covered
- **Model Outputs**: AI-generated inferences and predictions are considered personal data
- **Cloud AI Services**: Cloud-based AI services processing Montana resident data must comply
- **Purpose Limitation**: AI systems must operate within disclosed purposes

### Assessment Requirements

## Enforcement and Penalties

### Montana Attorney General
- **Enforcement Authority**: Montana Attorney General has exclusive enforcement authority
- **Investigation Powers**: Authority to investigate MCDPA violations
- **Civil Penalties**: Civil penalties for violations
- **Injunctive Relief**: Court orders to stop violations and ensure compliance

### Cure Period
- **60-Day Cure**: Businesses have 60 days to cure violations after notice
- **Good Faith Requirement**: Must make reasonable good faith efforts to cure
- **Penalty Protection**: Cure eliminates civil penalty liability for that violation
- **Documentation**: Must document cure efforts and results

### No Private Right of Action
- **State Enforcement Only**: Only Montana Attorney General can enforce MCDPA
- **No Consumer Lawsuits**: Consumers cannot sue directly under MCDPA
- **Other Legal Remedies**: Other laws may provide private enforcement options
- **Regulatory Approach**: Focus on regulatory rather than private enforcement

## Business Compliance Requirements

### Privacy Notice Requirements
- **Clear Disclosures**: Reasonably accessible and clear privacy notices
- **Data Categories**: Categories of personal data collected and processed
- **Processing Purposes**: Purposes for collecting and processing personal data
- **Third-Party Sharing**: Information about data sharing and disclosure
- **Consumer Rights**: Explanation of consumer rights and exercise procedures

### Consumer Request Processing
- **45-Day Response**: Must respond to consumer requests within 45 days
- **Identity Verification**: Reasonable procedures to verify consumer identity
- **Free Processing**: Must process requests without charge (with limited exceptions)
- **Appeal Rights**: Must provide process for consumers to appeal denials
- **Record Keeping**: Maintain records of consumer requests and responses

### Data Processing Requirements
- **Data Minimization**: Collect only data necessary for disclosed purposes
- **Purpose Limitation**: Process data only for disclosed and compatible purposes
- **Retention Limits**: Retain personal data only as long as necessary
- **Security Safeguards**: Implement appropriate technical and organizational safeguards
- **Processor Contracts**: Appropriate agreements with data processors
