# Iowa Consumer Data Protection Act (ICDPA)

## Overview
The Iowa Consumer Data Protection Act (ICDPA), enacted in 2023 and effective January 1, 2025, is Iowa's comprehensive consumer privacy law. ICDPA provides privacy protections for Iowa residents and follows the established framework of other state comprehensive privacy laws with some unique provisions.

## Key Provisions

### Consumer Rights
- **Right to Access**: Consumers can request access to personal data being processed
- **Right to Correct**: Consumers can correct inaccurate personal data
- **Right to Delete**: Consumers can request deletion of personal data
- **Right to Data Portability**: Consumers can obtain copies of personal data in portable format
- **Right to Opt-Out**: Consumers can opt out of targeted advertising, sale, and certain profiling

### Covered Entities
Businesses that:
- Conduct business in Iowa or produce products/services targeted to Iowa residents, AND
- During a calendar year, control or process personal data of at least 100,000 consumers, OR
- Control or process personal data of at least 25,000 consumers and derive over 50% of gross revenue from the sale of personal data

## AI and Automated Decision-Making

### Profiling and Automated Decisions
- **Opt-Out Rights**: Consumers can opt out of profiling for decisions with legal or similarly significant effects
- **Meaningful Information**: Right to meaningful information about automated decision-making logic
- **Human Review**: Right to human review and intervention in automated decisions
- **Transparency Requirements**: Clear explanations of automated processing and decision-making

### AI Development and Training
- **Training Data Protection**: Personal data used in AI model training must comply with ICDPA
- **Model Governance**: AI models processing Iowa resident data must meet compliance requirements
- **Inference Protection**: AI-generated inferences and predictions about consumers are covered
- **Purpose Limitation**: AI systems must operate within disclosed and legally compatible purposes

### Cloud AI Services
- **Service Provider Requirements**: Cloud AI services may qualify as processors under ICDPA
- **Contractual Obligations**: Specific contract requirements for AI service processors
- **Data Processing Agreements**: DPAs must include comprehensive ICDPA compliance provisions
- **International Processing**: Cross-border AI services must address ICDPA compliance requirements

## Data Protection Impact Assessments

### Required Assessments
Data protection impact assessments required for processing activities that present heightened risk:
- **Targeted Advertising**: Processing for behavioral advertising and targeting
- **Sale of Personal Data**: Processing involving sale or monetization of personal data
- **Profiling**: Profiling activities with legal or similarly significant effects on consumers
- **Sensitive Data**: Processing of sensitive personal data categories

### Assessment Requirements
- **Risk Analysis**: Comprehensive analysis of potential risks to consumer privacy
- **Impact Evaluation**: Evaluation of potential negative impacts and consequences
- **Benefit Assessment**: Assessment of benefits balanced against potential risks and harms
- **Mitigation Measures**: Implementation of appropriate safeguards and risk mitigation measures

## Enforcement and Penalties

### Iowa Attorney General
- **Enforcement Authority**: Iowa Attorney General has exclusive enforcement authority
- **Investigation Powers**: Authority to investigate ICDPA violations and compliance issues
- **Civil Penalties**: Civil penalties for violations up to $7,500 per violation
- **Injunctive Relief**: Authority to seek court orders for compliance and remediation

### Cure Period
- **90-Day Cure**: Businesses have 90 days to cure violations after written notice
- **Good Faith Requirement**: Must demonstrate reasonable good faith efforts to cure violations
- **Penalty Protection**: Successful cure eliminates civil penalty liability for that specific violation
- **Ongoing Monitoring**: Attorney General may require ongoing compliance verification

### No Private Right of Action
- **State Enforcement Only**: Only Iowa Attorney General can enforce ICDPA requirements
- **No Consumer Lawsuits**: Consumers cannot file private lawsuits directly under ICDPA
- **Other Legal Remedies**: Other state and federal laws may provide private enforcement mechanisms
- **Regulatory Focus**: Emphasis on state regulatory oversight and enforcement approach

## Business Compliance Requirements

### Privacy Notice Requirements
- **Accessible Format**: Privacy notices must be reasonably accessible and available to consumers
- **Clear Language**: Information must be presented in clear, plain, and understandable language
- **Data Categories**: Comprehensive description of categories of personal data collected and processed
- **Processing Purposes**: Specific and detailed purposes for personal data collection and processing
- **Third-Party Sharing**: Clear information about data sharing and disclosure to third parties
- **Consumer Rights**: Detailed explanation of consumer rights and procedures for exercising them

### Consumer Request Processing
- **45-Day Response**: Must respond to verified consumer requests within 45 days of receipt
- **Identity Verification**: Implement reasonable and appropriate procedures to verify consumer identity
- **Free Processing**: Must process consumer requests without charge (with limited exceptions)
- **Appeal Process**: Must provide accessible mechanism for consumers to appeal request denials
- **Record Keeping**: Maintain detailed records of all consumer requests and responses

### Data Processing Requirements
- **Data Minimization**: Collect only personal data that is necessary for disclosed purposes
- **Purpose Limitation**: Process personal data only for disclosed and compatible purposes
- **Retention Limits**: Retain personal data only for as long as reasonably necessary
- **Security Safeguards**: Implement appropriate technical and organizational security measures
- **Processor Agreements**: Execute compliant contracts with all data processors and service providers
