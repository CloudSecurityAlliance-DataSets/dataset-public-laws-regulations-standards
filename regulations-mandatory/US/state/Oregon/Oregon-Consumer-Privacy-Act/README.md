# Oregon Consumer Privacy Act (OCPA)

## Overview
The Oregon Consumer Privacy Act (OCPA), enacted in 2023 and effective July 1, 2024, is Oregon's comprehensive consumer privacy law. OCPA provides privacy protections for Oregon residents and follows the general model established by other state privacy laws while including some unique provisions.

## Key Provisions

### Consumer Rights
- **Right to Access**: Consumers can request access to personal data
- **Right to Correct**: Consumers can correct inaccurate personal data
- **Right to Delete**: Consumers can request deletion of personal data
- **Right to Data Portability**: Consumers can obtain copies of personal data in a portable format
- **Right to Opt-Out**: Consumers can opt out of targeted advertising, sale, and certain profiling

### Covered Entities
Businesses that:
- Conduct business in Oregon or produce products/services targeted to Oregon residents, AND
- During a calendar year, control or process personal data of at least 100,000 consumers, OR
- Control or process personal data of at least 25,000 consumers and derive over 25% of annual revenue from the sale of personal data

## AI and Automated Decision-Making

### Profiling and Automated Decisions
- **Opt-Out Rights**: Consumers can opt out of profiling for decisions with legal or similarly significant effects
- **Meaningful Information**: Right to meaningful information about automated decision-making logic
- **Human Review**: Right to human review of automated decisions
- **Transparency Requirements**: Controllers must provide clear explanations of automated processing

### AI Development and Training
- **Training Data Protection**: Personal data used in AI training must comply with OCPA
- **Model Governance**: AI models processing Oregon resident data must comply with requirements
- **Inference Protection**: AI-generated inferences and predictions about consumers are covered
- **Purpose Limitation**: AI systems must operate within disclosed and compatible purposes

### Cloud AI Services
- **Service Provider Requirements**: Cloud AI services may qualify as processors under OCPA
- **Contractual Obligations**: Specific contract requirements for AI service processors
- **Data Processing Agreements**: DPAs must comply with OCPA processor requirements
- **International Transfers**: Cross-border AI services raise compliance considerations

## Data Protection Impact Assessments

### Required Assessments
Data protection impact assessments required for processing that presents heightened risk, including:
- **Targeted Advertising**: Processing for behavioral advertising purposes
- **Sale of Personal Data**: Processing involving data sales or revenue sharing
- **Profiling**: Profiling with legal or similarly significant effects on consumers
- **Sensitive Data**: Processing of sensitive personal data categories

### Assessment Requirements
- **Risk Analysis**: Systematic analysis of processing risks to consumers
- **Impact Evaluation**: Evaluation of potential negative impacts on consumers
- **Benefit-Risk Balance**: Assessment of benefits weighed against potential harms
- **Mitigation Measures**: Implementation of safeguards to reduce identified risks

## Enforcement and Penalties

### Oregon Attorney General
- **Enforcement Authority**: Oregon Attorney General has exclusive enforcement authority
- **Investigation Powers**: Authority to investigate OCPA violations and compliance
- **Civil Penalties**: Civil penalties for violations up to $7,500 per violation
- **Injunctive Relief**: Court orders to stop violations and ensure ongoing compliance

### Cure Period
- **30-Day Cure**: Businesses have 30 days to cure violations after written notice
- **Good Faith Requirement**: Must make reasonable good faith efforts to cure violations
- **Penalty Protection**: Successful cure eliminates civil penalty liability for that violation
- **Ongoing Monitoring**: Attorney General may monitor compliance after cure period

### No Private Right of Action
- **State Enforcement Only**: Only Oregon Attorney General can enforce OCPA
- **No Consumer Lawsuits**: Consumers cannot file private lawsuits under OCPA
- **Other Legal Remedies**: Other state and federal laws may provide private enforcement
- **Regulatory Focus**: Emphasis on state regulatory oversight and enforcement

## Business Compliance Requirements

### Privacy Notice Requirements
- **Accessible Notices**: Privacy notices must be reasonably accessible to consumers
- **Clear Information**: Clear and understandable information about data practices
- **Data Categories**: Categories of personal data collected and processed
- **Processing Purposes**: Specific purposes for personal data processing
- **Third-Party Sharing**: Information about data sharing and disclosure practices
- **Consumer Rights**: Clear explanation of consumer rights and exercise procedures

### Consumer Request Processing
- **45-Day Response**: Must respond to consumer requests within 45 days of receipt
- **Identity Verification**: Reasonable procedures to verify consumer identity and authority
- **Free Processing**: Must process requests without charge to consumers (with limited exceptions)
- **Appeal Process**: Must provide process for consumers to appeal request denials
- **Documentation**: Maintain records of consumer requests and responses

### Data Processing Requirements
- **Data Minimization**: Collect only personal data necessary for disclosed purposes
- **Purpose Limitation**: Process personal data only for disclosed and compatible purposes
- **Retention Limits**: Retain personal data only as long as reasonably necessary
- **Security Safeguards**: Implement appropriate technical and organizational security measures
- **Processor Agreements**: Execute appropriate contracts with data processors
