# Indiana Consumer Data Protection Act (INCDPA)

## Overview
The Indiana Consumer Data Protection Act (INCDPA), enacted in 2023 and effective January 1, 2026, is Indiana's comprehensive consumer privacy law. INCDPA provides privacy protections for Indiana residents and follows the established model of other state comprehensive privacy laws while including provisions specifically relevant to Indiana's manufacturing and healthcare sectors.

## Key Provisions

### Consumer Rights
- **Right to Access**: Consumers can request access to personal data
- **Right to Correct**: Consumers can correct inaccurate personal data
- **Right to Delete**: Consumers can request deletion of personal data
- **Right to Data Portability**: Consumers can obtain copies of personal data
- **Right to Opt-Out**: Consumers can opt out of targeted advertising, sale, and profiling

### Covered Entities
Businesses that:
- Conduct business in Indiana or produce products/services targeted to Indiana residents, AND
- During a calendar year, control or process personal data of at least 100,000 consumers, OR
- Control or process personal data of at least 25,000 consumers and derive over 50% of gross revenue from the sale of personal data

## Enforcement and Penalties

### Indiana Attorney General
- **Enforcement Authority**: Indiana Attorney General has exclusive enforcement authority
- **Investigation Powers**: Authority to investigate INCDPA violations
- **Civil Penalties**: Civil penalties for violations
- **Injunctive Relief**: Court orders to stop violations and ensure compliance

### Cure Period
- **30-Day Cure**: Businesses have 30 days to cure violations after notice
- **Good Faith Requirement**: Must make reasonable good faith efforts to cure
- **Penalty Protection**: Cure eliminates civil penalty liability for that violation
- **Sectoral Considerations**: Special considerations for manufacturing and healthcare sectors

### No Private Right of Action
- **State Enforcement Only**: Only Indiana Attorney General can enforce INCDPA
- **No Consumer Lawsuits**: Consumers cannot sue directly under INCDPA
- **Other Legal Remedies**: Other laws may provide private enforcement options
- **Regulatory Approach**: Focus on regulatory rather than private enforcement

## Business Compliance Requirements

### Privacy Notice Requirements
- **Clear Disclosures**: Reasonably accessible and clear privacy notices
- **Data Categories**: Categories of personal data collected and processed
- **Processing Purposes**: Purposes for collecting and processing personal data
- **Third-Party Sharing**: Information about data sharing and disclosure
- **Consumer Rights**: Explanation of consumer rights and exercise procedures
- **Sectoral Disclosures**: Specific disclosures for manufacturing and healthcare data

### Consumer Request Processing
- **45-Day Response**: Must respond to consumer requests within 45 days
- **Identity Verification**: Reasonable procedures to verify consumer identity
- **Free Processing**: Must process requests without charge (with limited exceptions)
- **Appeal Rights**: Must provide process for consumers to appeal denials
- **Record Keeping**: Maintain records of consumer requests and responses

### Data Processing Requirements
- **Data Minimization**: Collect only data necessary for disclosed purposes
- **Purpose Limitation**: Process data only for disclosed and compatible purposes
- **Retention Limits**: Retain personal data only as long as necessary
- **Security Safeguards**: Implement appropriate technical and organizational safeguards
- **Processor Contracts**: Appropriate agreements with data processors
