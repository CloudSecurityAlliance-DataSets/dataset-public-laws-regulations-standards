# Cross-Sector Cybersecurity Performance Goals

A common set of protections that all critical infrastructure entities - from
large to small - should implement to meaningfully reduce the likelihood and
impact of known risks and adversary techniques.

CISA's Cross-Sector Cybersecurity Performance Goals 2.0 (CPGs) are a subset of
cybersecurity practices, selected through a thorough process of industry,
government, and expert consultation, aimed at meaningfully reducing risks to
both critical infrastructure operations and the American people. These
voluntary Cross-Sector CPGs strive to help small- and medium-sized
organizations kickstart their cybersecurity efforts by prioritizing investment
in a limited number of essential actions with high-impact security outcomes.

### **Cross-Sector Cybersecurity Performance Goals 2.0:**

[View All CPGs](/cybersecurity-performance-goals-2-0-cpg-2-0 "View All CPGs")

##### **The Cross-Sector CPGs are intended to be:**

  * A baseline set of cybersecurity practices broadly applicable across critical infrastructure with known risk-reduction value. 

  * A benchmark for critical infrastructure operators to measure and improve their cybersecurity maturity. 

  * A combination of recommended practices for information technology and operational technology owners, including a prioritized set of security practices. 

  * Unique from other control frameworks as they consider not only the practices that address risk to individual entities, but also the aggregate risk to the nation. 

##### **Key Updates to the Cross-Sector Cybersecurity Performance Goals**

CISA's Cross-Sector CPGs 2.0 have been updated to align to the [ _National
Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST) Cybersecurity Framework (CSF)
2.0_](https://www.nist.gov/cyberframework) functions and the evolving
cybersecurity threat landscape. The updated Cross-Sector CPGs 2.0 build upon
the foundation established in version 1.0.1.

  * **Enhanced Alignment with NIST CSF 2.0** : The CPGs have been updated to reflect the latest cybersecurity best practices and frameworks with the addition of the GOVERN function. The GOVERN function regroups existing goals and creates new goals integrating leadership accountability, oversight, and risk management, mirroring NIST CSF 2.0’s new emphasis on organizational governance.
  * **Goal Consolidation - Streamlining & Cross-Sector Alignment**: Operational Technology (OT) & Information Technology (IT) goals from CPG 1.0.1 are addressed holistically through new shared goals. Small- and medium-sized entities can apply one framework across their entire estate, without confusion over domain-specific goals.
  * **Net-New Goals - Addressing Emerging Gaps & Threats**: New goals have been added to address emerging threats and include areas such as cybersecurity oversight, risks associated with managed service providers (MSPs), principle of least privileges, and incident communication procedures.
  * **Deletions & Intent Preservation: ** CPGs 2.0 removes three duplicative goals based on real-world usage data and practitioner feedback. The original goals’ outcomes are now embedded in other goals.
  * **Methodology & Documentation Enhancements: **The CPG 2.0 Report now includes improved Cost, Impact, and Ease of Implementation ratings for each goal. The definitions and logic for each rating are also included in the CPGs 2.0 Report to aid assessors in conducting CPG assessments with a greater degree of repeatable analytic consistency.
  * **New CSET Assessment Module:** A new CSET module will be available in Q1 2026 for CPG 2.0, inclusive of the new set of goals and implementation scales. An accompanying updated CPG 2.0 Checklist will also be available at that time.

##### **Sector Specific Goals**

CISA is proud to introduce the first sets of Sector-Specific Goals (SSGs) that
are tailored for organizations in select critical infrastructure sectors.
Developed in partnership with Sector Risk Management Agencies (SRMAs) and
sector stakeholders, SSGs address unique requirements in select critical
infrastructure sectors, and build upon CISA’s [ _Cross-Sector
CPGs_](https://www.cisa.gov/news-events/alerts/2023/03/21/cisa-releases-
updated-cybersecurity-performance-goals).

##### **Available Now:**

  * Cross-Sector CPGs
  * Chemical Sector SSGs
  * Energy Sector (Distribution and Distributed Energy Resources) SSGs
  * Healthcare SSGs
  * Information Technology SSGs

##### **Coming Soon:**

  * Financial Services SSGs (Winter 2025)
