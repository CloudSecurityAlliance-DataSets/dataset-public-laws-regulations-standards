## Australia’s plan for AI

[ __ Link copied ](#australias-plan-for-ai "Copy link to this section")

The Australian Government’s ambition is to harness AI technologies to create a
fairer, stronger Australia where every person shares the benefits.

AI is reshaping the global economy and how Australians work, learn and
connect. It can:

  * close service gaps in health, disability and aged care
  * improve education and employment opportunities
  * create secure, well-paid jobs in future industries
  * create an environment for innovation. 

To remain competitive, productive and resilient, we need coordinated action to
seize the potential of AI while managing its risks.

The National AI Plan sets out the government’s direction on AI. It focusses
on:

  * capturing the opportunity
  * spreading the benefits
  * keeping Australians safe.

### [National AI Plan](/publications/national-ai-plan)

2 December 2025

A whole-of-government framework to ensure technology works for everyone. It
will guide government, industry, research and communities to work with AI.__

## Capturing the AI opportunity

[ __ Link copied ](#capturing-the-ai-opportunity "Copy link to this section")

We are capturing the opportunity for Australia to become a global leader in
developing and adopting trusted, secure and responsible AI.

  * ### [National AI Centre __](/node/93610)

NAIC is the government’s lead body supporting industry to unlock the economic
benefits of AI.

  * ### [Kicking off the AI Accelerator initiative __](/news/ai-accelerator-initiative-kicks-funding-industry-led-research)

Funding rounds of the Cooperative Research Centres (CRC) Program will support
AI development and commercialisation by businesses and researchers across
Australia.

  * ### [Setting expectations for data centres __](/publications/expectations-data-centres-and-ai-infrastructure-developers)

The government’s expectations will help incentivise investment in data centres
that align with Australia’s interest.

  * ### [Collaborating with leading AI and technology companies __](/science-technology-and-innovation/technology/artificial-intelligence/collaborating-ai-and-technology-companies)

The government is establishing arrangements with leading companies to
strengthen our national AI capabilities as part of the National AI Plan.

##  Spreading the benefits

[ __ Link copied ](#spreading-the-benefits "Copy link to this section")

Every Australian should be able to benefit from AI, regardless of age,
location or gender. We are spreading the benefits of AI by supporting
adoption. AI adoption has the potential to improve business productivity and
deliver better wages, job satisfaction and stability for workers.

  * ### [AI Adopt Program __(external link)](https://www.ai.gov.au/about/connect-us/ai-adopt-centres)

The program funds AI adopt centres to help small and medium businesses use AI
in a practical and responsible way.

  * ### [Guidance for AI Adoption __(external link)](https://ai.gov.au/staying-safe-and-responsible/essential-ai-practices/guidance-ai-adoption-implementation-guidance)

This practical resource from the National AI Centre helps Australian business
adopt AI safely and responsibly. It evolves the Voluntary AI Safety Standard
and Australia’s AI Ethics Principles.

##  Keeping Australians safe

[ __ Link copied ](#keeping-australians-safe "Copy link to this section")

To protect Australians and maintain trust and confidence in AI systems, we’re
committed to understanding and responding to advanced AI.

  * ### [Artificial Intelligence Safety Institute __](/news/australia-establish-new-institute-strengthen-ai-safety)

The government is establishing an Australian AI Safety Institute to monitor,
test and share information on emerging AI capabilities, risks and harms.

  * ### [AI safety science __](/science-technology-and-innovation/technology/artificial-intelligence/ai-safety-science)

We’re progressing national and international knowledge on AI capabilities,
risks and harms.

##  Partnering internationally on responsible AI

[ __ Link copied ](#partnering-internationally-on-responsible-ai "Copy link to
this section")

Australia engages internationally on AI to support Australian innovation and
to shape international AI governance.

  * ### [Paris AI Action Summit statement __](/news/australia-signs-paris-ai-action-summit-statement)

The Statement on Inclusive and Sustainable Artificial Intelligence for People
and the Planet builds on the work of the Bletchley and Seoul Declarations.

  * ### [Global Partnership on Artificial Intelligence (GPAI)__](/news/australia-supports-safe-ai-through-new-gpai-declaration)

Australia is collaborating on the responsible use and development of AI as a
founding member of GPAI.

  * ### [EU-Australia Digital Economy and Technology Policy Dialogue __](/news/australia-and-european-union-deepen-digital-relationship)

Australia and the European Union are building on our existing relationship to
strengthen ties on priority digital and technology issues.

  * ### [Hiroshima AI Process Friends Group __](/news/australia-joins-hiroshima-ai-process-friends-group)

Australia is part of the Hiroshima AI Process Friends Group. The group
broadens the Hiroshima AI Process beyond G7 members.

  * ### [Seoul Declaration __](/publications/seoul-declaration-countries-attending-ai-seoul-summit-21-22-may-2024)

Australia and 9 other countries signed the declaration. It follows the
Bletchley Declaration and confirms a shared understanding of the opportunities
and risks posed by AI.

  * ### [Bletchley Declaration on AI safety __](/publications/bletchley-declaration-countries-attending-ai-safety-summit-1-2-november-2023)

Australia and 27 other countries signed the declaration at the AI Safety
Summit in the United Kingdom.
